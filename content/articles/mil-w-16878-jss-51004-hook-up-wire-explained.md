# MIL-W-16878 & JSS 51004 Hook-Up Wire Explained: Types ET, E, EE, Ratings & Selection

If you specify high-temperature hook-up wire, two standards come up again and again: the US military specification **MIL-W-16878** (now MIL-DTL-16878, also dual-rated to NEMA HP3) and its Indian equivalent **JSS 51004**. Both define unshielded wire for the internal hook-up and lead wiring of electrical and electronic equipment. This guide explains what the standards cover, how the PTFE "Type" designations (ET, E, EE) differ, and how to turn a requirement into a complete part call-out.

## What MIL-W-16878 / JSS 51004 actually specify

The specifications cover **unshielded single-conductor hook-up and lead wire** for electronics and equipment. Across the family, the temperature rating spans roughly **−65 °C to +260 °C** and voltage ratings run from **250 V to 5,000 V RMS**, depending on the construction. They define the conductor (material, plating, stranding), the insulation material and wall, dimensional tolerances, and the electrical tests the wire must pass.

The original spec uses letter "types" (B, C, D for PVC; **E, EE, ET for PTFE**); the modern NEMA HP3 designations map onto these, though most of the industry still refers to the classic "16878" types.

## The PTFE types: ET, E and EE

The three PTFE/Teflon types differ mainly by **voltage class and insulation wall thickness** — thicker insulation withstands higher voltage.

| Type | Max operating voltage (AC RMS) | Dielectric test | Spark test | Typical use |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **ET** | 250 V | 1,500 V | 2,500 V | Thin-wall, dense, low-voltage internal wiring |
| **E** | 600 V | 2,000 V | 3,400 V | General high-temperature equipment & harness wiring |
| **EE** | 1,000 V | 3,000 V | 5,000 V | Higher-voltage, protected high-temperature applications |

All three use PTFE insulation over a copper conductor and share the wide thermal envelope. As a rule of thumb: **ET** where space is tight and voltage is low, **E** for most general high-temperature wiring, and **EE** where voltage headroom matters.

## Conductors: plating and temperature

The conductor plating sets the upper temperature and the solderability:

- **Silver-plated copper (SPC)** — excellent solderability and conductivity; commonly rated to about **+200 °C**.
- **Nickel-plated copper (NPC)** — higher temperature capability, to about **+260 °C**; preferred for the hottest environments.
- **Silver-plated copper-clad steel / high-strength alloy** — where extra tensile strength is needed.

Conductors are supplied solid or stranded (1, 7, 19, 37, 133 strands are standard), in sizes from about **AWG 32 down to AWG 6/4**. More strands means more flexibility and flex-life for the same gauge.

## Standard colour code (NEMA HP3 / M16878)

PTFE hook-up wire is offered in solid colours and stripe combinations using a standard numeric code:

`0 Black · 1 Brown · 2 Red · 3 Orange · 4 Yellow · 5 Green · 6 Blue · 7 Violet · 8 Grey · 9 White` (plus **Pink** and **Natural**).

Stripes are written as base + stripe digits — e.g. a white wire with one black and one brown stripe is coded `-901`.

## How to write a complete specification

A complete hook-up-wire call-out answers six questions:

1. **Standard / type** — e.g. MIL-W-16878E Type E (or JSS 51004 equivalent)
2. **AWG size** — e.g. 22 AWG
3. **Stranding** — e.g. 19/34 (19 strands of 34 AWG)
4. **Conductor plating** — SPC or NPC
5. **Voltage class** — ET / E / EE
6. **Colour / stripe** — from the code above

Example: *MIL-W-16878E, Type E, 22 AWG, 19/34 stranded, silver-plated copper, white with black/brown stripe (-901).* That string is unambiguous to any qualified manufacturer.

## The JSS equivalents you'll see together

Indian defence and electronics procurement typically references the JSS series alongside the US MIL specs:

- **JSS 51004** — equipment / hook-up wires (≈ MIL-W-16878E)
- **JSS 51100** — co-axial cables (≈ MIL-C-17)
- **JSS 54802** — PTFE sleeves (≈ MIL-I-22129)

Manufacturing to both the JSS and MIL specifications means a single part can satisfy domestic defence and international requirements.

### FAQ
**What is MIL-W-16878?** A US military specification for unshielded hook-up/lead wire, rated −65 to +260 °C and 250–5,000 V; PTFE versions are Types E, EE and ET.
**What's the difference between Type E and Type EE wire?** Voltage class — Type E is rated 600 V, Type EE 1,000 V (thicker insulation).
**Is JSS 51004 the same as MIL-W-16878?** They are equivalent standards for PTFE hook-up wire used in Indian and US procurement respectively.
**What temperature can MIL-W-16878 PTFE wire handle?** Up to about +260 °C for nickel-plated constructions; ~+200 °C for silver-plated.
